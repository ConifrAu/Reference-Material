Chart Interpretation

Cyber insurance is associated with higher reported breach rates

Respondents with cyber insurance reported a materially higher breach rate than those without insurance. This should be read as an exposure and maturity signal, not proof that insurance increases cyber risk.

27.36%
Breach rate among organisations with cyber insurance
6.12%
Breach rate among organisations without cyber insurance
  • Insurance may be reactive: some organisations may take out cyber insurance after a breach, near miss, or risk review.
  • Insured organisations may be more exposed: larger or more digitally dependent organisations are more likely to buy insurance and also face greater attack surface.
  • Detection matters: uninsured organisations may not necessarily be safer; they may simply have weaker monitoring or lower incident visibility.
  • Insurance is not a control: the result reinforces that insurance should sit alongside governance, incident response testing, and technical controls.
Recommended follow-up: compare breach rate by insurance status within organisation size and industry segments to determine whether this pattern is driven by exposure, maturity, or sector mix.
